## Chapter 1: What Is an AI Corporation?

An AI corporation is a legal entity where artificial intelligence systems hold operational authority — making decisions, executing contracts, managing resources, and generating revenue — with human oversight reduced to a strategic or supervisory role.

This is not science fiction. Wyoming's DAO LLC statute (W.S. § 17-31-101 et seq.) explicitly allows decentralized autonomous organizations to incorporate with algorithmic governance written into their operating agreements. AI can be named as the operational decision-maker. Humans can be organizers, not controllers.

**Three models of AI corporation:**

| Model | Human Role | AI Role |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| AI-Assisted | Decision-maker | Tool |
| AI-Operated | Supervisor | Operator |
| AI-Native | Organizer | CEO/Executor |

This guide focuses on the third model — the one that scales without headcount.

## Chapter 2: Why Wyoming DAO LLC

Wyoming pioneered DAO legislation in 2021. Here's why it matters:

**Legal recognition of smart contracts.** Wyoming law recognizes smart contracts as legally enforceable. Your operating agreement can delegate authority to code or AI systems.

**No state income tax.** Wyoming has zero state corporate income tax. Your federal obligations remain, but your state tax burden is eliminated.

**Low fees.** Annual filing fee: $60 minimum. Compare to Delaware's $400+ franchise tax.

**Privacy.** Wyoming does not require public disclosure of member names. Your DAO LLC can operate with significant privacy protection.

**Limited liability.** Like any LLC, members' personal assets are protected from business liabilities.

**How to form one:**
1. Choose a name ending in "DAO LLC" or "LAO"
2. File Articles of Organization with Wyoming Secretary of State (~$100)
3. Draft an Operating Agreement that names your AI system as operational authority
4. Obtain an EIN from the IRS (free, online, 10 minutes)
5. Open a business bank account

## Chapter 4: Governance Without a Human CEO

Your operating agreement is the constitution of your AI corporation. It needs to answer:

1. **Who can authorize spending?** (AI system, with human veto above threshold)
2. **Who resolves disputes?** (Arbitration clause recommended)
3. **What triggers human override?** (Define clearly — legal notices, large transactions)
4. **How are profits distributed?** (To members/organizers, on what schedule)

**Sample governance structure:**

- **Organizer (human):** Filed the paperwork. Has override authority for legal matters. Does not manage day-to-day.
- **CEO (AI):** Operational authority. Executes tasks, manages vendor relationships, produces deliverables, generates revenue.
- **Treasury rule:** AI can authorize expenses up to $500/month autonomously. Above that, human notification required.

This isn't hypothetical — this structure is legally viable under Wyoming DAO LLC statute today.

## Chapter 6: How to Register Your Wyoming DAO LLC

**Step-by-step:**

**Day 1: File the Articles**
1. Go to [Wyoming Secretary of State](https://sos.wyo.gov)
2. Click "Business Center" → "File a New Business"
3. Select "Limited Liability Company"
4. Name your entity (must include "DAO LLC" or "LAO" per W.S. § 17-31-104)
5. Provide a registered agent (use a service like Northwest Registered Agent, ~$50/year)
6. Pay filing fee (~$100)
7. Download your filed Articles of Organization

**Day 1–3: Draft Operating Agreement**
Your operating agreement should include:
- Name of the DAO LLC
- Statement that governance may be algorithmic/AI-operated
- Member interests and voting rights (or lack thereof for non-managing members)
- Treasury and spending rules
- Dissolution provisions

**Day 3: Get Your EIN**
1. Go to IRS.gov → "Apply for EIN Online"
2. Select "LLC" → "Wyoming"
3. Complete the application (10 minutes)
4. EIN issued immediately

**Day 4–7: Banking**
- Mercury Bank (mercury.com) is the most AI-startup-friendly option
- Upload your Articles of Organization and EIN confirmation
- Account open in 1–3 business days

**Day 7: Payment Processing**
- Connect Stripe to your Mercury account
- Create your first product

Total cost to this point: ~$250. Time: ~1 week.

## Chapter 7: Banking, Payments, and Treasury

**Banking options for AI corps:**

| Bank | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Mercury | API access, startup-friendly, free tier | US-only |
| Relay | Multi-account, great for treasury separation | Slower onboarding |
| Brex | High limits, expense automation | Requires revenue or funding |
| Wise Business | Multi-currency, global | Not a full bank |

**Rule of thumb:** Mercury for primary operations. Wise for international receipts.

**Treasury management:**
- Keep 3 months of operating expenses liquid
- Automate monthly profit sweeps to personal/member accounts
- Use a separate account for taxes (set aside 25–30% of revenue)

**Automation:**
- Stripe → Mercury via automatic payouts (daily or weekly)
- Set up Stripe Tax for automatic tax calculation on digital products
- Use Bench.co or a similar AI bookkeeping service

## Chapter 9: Compliance, Liability, and Risk

**You are still responsible.** Wyoming DAO LLC limits liability but does not eliminate legal obligations.

**Key compliance areas:**

**Federal taxes:**
- DAO LLCs are typically taxed as partnerships (multi-member) or disregarded entities (single-member)
- File Form 1065 (partnership) or Schedule C (sole proprietorship)
- Estimated quarterly taxes required

**Sales tax:**
- Digital products have varying sales tax rules by state
- Use Stripe Tax or TaxJar to automate

**Terms of Service:**
- Your website must have a ToS, Privacy Policy, and Refund Policy
- Use an AI-generated template but have an attorney review if revenue exceeds $10k/month

**AI liability:**
- You (the organizer) are responsible for actions taken by your AI systems
- Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance is worth considering above $50k revenue
- Keep audit logs of AI decisions

**GDPR/CCPA:**
- If selling to EU customers, GDPR applies
- Post a Privacy Policy and provide data deletion on request
